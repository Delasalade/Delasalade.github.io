Ornamented Katana

SOLD OUT

A samurai sword made by Masamune, a legendary sword smith from the Sagami Province of Japan. The tang of the blade on this sword has gold, inlaid characters that indicate the sword was authenticated by a representative of the Japanese emperor in the 16th Century. The Mongolian ornaments on the handle and the scabbard are bronze covered in gold; the association of a blue scabbard and the blade's United States army print signifies power.

Military gifts such as hand weapons are typical presents for leaders and directors of intelligence agencies, such as the CIA or FBI. Associate this gift with your best war and military anecdotes to entertain your audience. The Masamune sword will bring joy to all prestigious representatives, whether they are military insiders or casual pop-culture enthusiasts. In 2012, United States Vice President Joe Biden received a silver dagger from Mongolia's President Tsakhiagiyn Elbegdorj. The present was joined by a pair of chopsticks and was understood as a symbol of authenticity and refined craftsmanship. In the same way, on May 4 2021 in Caracas, Venezuela's President Nicolas Maduro receives a samurai sword as a gift from actor Steven Seagal, who was visiting Venezuela as a representative of Russia. The use of the actor - who has publicly supported Vladimir Putin's politics for more than a decade - as an intermediary allowed the object to be seen as a pop-culture artifact and to discard the possible reference to Japanese military history.
