Tartan woven textile is appreciated worldwide for its versatility; from New York to Kyoto, you will find tartans on shirts, skirts, jackets, and a large variety of objects. Since the beginning of the 20th century, far after the international re-appropriation of the tartan pattern, Scotland has started to use tartan as a tool for soft power worldwide. Scotland's Register of Tartans is now open to entries worldwide and new unique national tartans are designed for allied countries. In 2006, Scotland offered France and Japan their own national tartans, respectively to commemorate the 1295 Auld Alliance and the 1959 rise of the first Scottish samurai Thomas Blake Glover.
